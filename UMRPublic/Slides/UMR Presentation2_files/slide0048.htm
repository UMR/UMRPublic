Universal Medical Records (UMR)
—Universal Medical Records (UMR) is a secure, independent software solution for capturing medical information and delivering it to healthcare providers and their patients.
—The UMR information network - actually, a network of networks - will provide a universally accessible data capture and authentication system for all patients and consumers, regardless of where they get their medical care, who pays for it, where they work, or where they live over the lifetime of the patient.
—UMR EMR is an innovative electronic medical records system designed to help physician practices improve quality of care, reduce risk, cut costs, and increase revenues. Appropriate for any sized medical practice—from a small, solo practice to a large, multi-provider/multi-location group—this feature-rich system allows you to create and maintain complete medical records faster and more efficiently than ever before.
—
—UMR is the health care industry's equivalent of a highly specialized, hyper-secure Internet. UMR is the end-result of several years of research and development work with one common, overarching goal: to jumpstart computerized data integration for the U.S. health care system.
