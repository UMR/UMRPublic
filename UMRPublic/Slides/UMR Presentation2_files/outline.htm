Universal Medical Records (UMR)
  • Universal Medical Records (UMR) is a secure, independent software solution for capturing medical information and delivering it to healthcare providers and their patients.
  • The UMR information network - actually, a network of networks - will provide a universally accessible data capture and authentication system for all patients and consumers, regardless of where they get their medical care, who pays for it, where they work, or where they live over the lifetime of the patient.
  • UMR EMR is an innovative electronic medical records system designed to help physician practices improve quality of care, reduce risk, cut costs, and increase revenues. Appropriate for any sized medical practice—from a small, solo practice to a large, multi-provider/multi-location group—this feature-rich system allows you to create and maintain complete medical records faster and more efficiently than ever before.


  • UMR is the health care industry's equivalent of a highly specialized, hyper-secure Internet. UMR is the end-result of several years of research and development work with one common, overarching goal: to jumpstart computerized data integration for the U.S. health care system.

Technology -- Connectivity
  • Application Service Provider (ASP)
    • In addition to being offered as a client/server application, UMR can also be implemented as an ASP (Application Service Provider) application. This configuration brings UMR Enterprise into a practice via an Internet VPN (Virtual Private Network), eliminating the need to implement or maintain application and database servers.
    • The applications can be accessed at any workstation that has Internet access and the VPN client installed. Both encryption (triple DES) and secure authentication (SecurID) are part of the ASP offering.
  • Connection with your own in-house systems
    • UMR can be fully integrated with your in-house clinical infomation management system, Laboratory, or other ancillary clinical data sources. The system is smart enough to flag abnormal results and update all the appropriate health maintenance items.

ASP -- Technology

  • Broadband Internet Connection:
    • To access UMR ASP from the medical office, users need to have a broadband internet connection. We highly recommend a dedicated, always-on Internet connection such as a fractional or full T1. A broadband connection such as DSL (Digital Subscriber Line) or cable may also be an option.
  • Data security
    • Virtual Private Network (VPN) architecture, data communication travels through a secured pathway from and to each site. The VPN connections are encrypted using triple DES encryption and authenticated using RSA's SecurID protocols, with firewalls at both ends of the connection.
  • Data Encryption:
    • We employ triple DES encryption to protect privacy while information is in transit across the Internet.
  • RSA SecurID:
    • UMR ASP employs the latest SecurID authentication technology that sets up a barrier that is much stronger than a conventional password system. It combines something a users knows (a five digit PIN) with an unique six digit code that changes every 60 seconds and is specific to the user and the time. Each user is given a key-chain like device that generates the new codes continuously for three years. Only when these two authentication factors are validated is access granted.
  • Firewall Technology:
    • The firewall protects your data at both ends of the link. At the ASP end, it authenticates all users and prevents intrusions. At the VPN client end, it protects your individual workstation.

Privacy and Security
  • Those on UMR 's technical staff who are developing authentication subsystems come not from health care, but from the banking industry, and bring with them not just best practices, but a culture of zero tolerance for security breaches.
  • Because UMR will be connected to data networks that exist today behind the curtain of the health care system - and because of the sophisticated and rigorous security and patient record location intelligence included in the Common Framework design - the user of a personal health information product will be able to quickly access his or her information. The information that will pass through the UMR network into the patient's control will be completely de-identified, assuring privacy in the extremely unlikely event that it is not the patient's actual medical data. Furthermore, in keeping with the principles of the Common Framework - and as a final way of ensuring data integrity - the user will have the ability to review that data and choose to include or exclude it from their record.
  • Despite all our efforts to build the broadest possible network, a tremendous amount of our medical information will remain on paper for years to come. As a result, UMR will also allow patients and consumers to capture and store scanned images of any documents they feel are important and may one day want to share with their caregivers.
